About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. student in Geoinformatics and Earth Observation Laboratory (GEOlab) advised by Prof. Guido Cervone at Pennsylvania State University with a doctoral minor in Social Data Analytics (SoDA). Before that, I joined the (GeoVISTA Center) under the supervision of Prof. Donna Peuquet and obtained my second Master degree of Geography in 2018. My research foci include two main areas: 1) spatiotemporal information diffusion, natural language processing, social network analysis; and 2) hyperspectral image analysis, deep learning algorithm developments for the target detection.

In Fall 2014, I got admitted to the University of Pennsylvania for the M.Sc. degree of Urban Spatial Analytics in the School of Design. My research foci include the data science, spatial data analytics - most notably Geographic Information System (GIS) - statistics, R and Python programming, data visualisation and web-based mapping with substantive knowledge under the urban context (e.g., urban land use planning, transportation, natural hazard simulations and predictions).

I received my B.Sc. of Surveying and Mapping from Wuhan University in June 2014. The topic of my bachelor thesis is - Reconstruction of 3D City Model based on oblique images.




Working Experience
